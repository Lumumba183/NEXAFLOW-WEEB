Website Pricing in Kenya: The Complete Breakdown

Before we dive into numbers, let's clarify what "website cost" actually includes. Many agencies quote a low price but charge extra for essentials like domain registration, hosting, SSL certificates, or even contact forms. Here's what's typically included:

Component What It Is Typical Cost (KES)
Domain Name Your website address (e.g., yourbusiness.co.ke) 1,000 – 2,000/year
Web Hosting Server space to store your website files 2,000 – 8,000/year
SSL Certificate Security certificate (HTTPS padlock) Free – 5,000/year
Design & Development Building the actual website 5,000 – 100,000+
Content Creation Writing text, sourcing images 1,500 – 5,000/page
Maintenance Updates, security, backups 2,000 – 5,000/year

Hidden Costs to Watch For

We've seen too many Kenyan business owners get surprised by costs that weren't mentioned upfront. Here's what to ask about:

1. Domain Renewal

Your domain (e.g., yourbusiness.co.ke) needs to be renewed every year. Some agencies charge KSH 2,000–3,000 for renewal when it only costs KSH 1,000–1,500 directly. We include the first year free and charge only KSH 1,500/year for renewal.

2. Hosting Quality

Cheap hosting (KSH 1,000/year) means slow loading speeds, frequent downtime, and poor security. For a business website, budget at least KSH 3,000–5,000/year for reliable hosting. We use Netlify's global CDN for fast loading worldwide.

3. Content Creation

Do you have your own text and images, or does the agency need to create them? Professional copywriting costs KSH 1,500–3,000 per page. Stock photos are free, but custom photography costs more. We guide you on providing content, or we can create it for you at KSH 1,500/page.

4. Maintenance & Updates

Websites need regular updates for security, plugin updates, and content changes. Some agencies charge KSH 5,000+/month for this. Our annual maintenance is only KSH 2,000/year — or KSH 2,500–5,000/month for enhanced maintenance with priority support.

5. E-commerce Add-ons

If you want to sell online, you'll need M-Pesa integration, product pages, a cart system, and order management. This typically adds KSH 10,000–30,000 to the base cost. We specialize in Shopify stores with M-Pesa integration.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a 5-page website cost in Kenya?

A 5-page website in Kenya typically costs between KSH 6,500 and KSH 18,000 depending on features. At NexaFlow Digital, our Basic Package (5 pages) starts at KSH 6,500 including contact form, image gallery, social media integration, responsive design, and a free domain.

Are there hidden costs when building a website in Kenya?

Common hidden costs include domain renewal (KSH 1,000–2,000/year), hosting (KSH 2,000–5,000/year), SSL certificate, content creation, and ongoing maintenance. At NexaFlow Digital, we include a free domain and professional email in all packages. Annual maintenance is KSH 2,000/year.

How long does it take to build a website in Kenya?

A basic 5-page website typically takes 1–2 weeks. A 10-page standard website takes 2–3 weeks. Premium websites with CMS integration take 3–4 weeks. Timeline depends on how quickly you provide content (text, images, logo) and feedback.

Do I need to pay monthly for my website?

After the initial build cost, you'll only pay for domain renewal (KSH 1,500/year) and hosting (included in our maintenance). Our basic maintenance is KSH 2,000/year. There are no mandatory monthly fees unless you choose enhanced maintenance or additional services.

Can I accept M-Pesa payments on my website?

Yes, but it requires e-commerce functionality which is typically an add-on. We specialize in Shopify stores with M-Pesa integration. For basic brochure websites, we can add a "Pay via M-Pesa" button that redirects to your Till Number or Paybill.
